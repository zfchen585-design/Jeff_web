About

I am Zhaofu (Jeff) Chen, an undergraduate researcher at the University of California, Irvine, pursuing a B.S. in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering with a minor in Materials Science and Engineering. My research sits at the intersection of complex oxide synthesis, thin-film processing, defect chemistry, and electrochemical/electrical characterization.

My recent work focuses on amorphous high-entropy and complex concentrated oxides for CO2 electroreduction, indium-oxide-based medium-entropy oxides, PLD-grown thin films, oxygen-vacancy engineering, and data workflows that turn experimental batches into publication-quality figures and decisions.

Current Projects

Amorphous High-Entropy Oxides for CO2 Electroreduction

Correlating amorphous structure, oxygen-vacancy chemistry, EIS response, and 4-probe conductivity across complex oxide thin films.

Indium-Oxide-Based Medium-Entropy Oxides

Mapping composition, processing, and phase stability using XRD indexing, quantitative phase analysis, and batch comparison workflows.

Oxygen-Vacancy Engineering in InSnZnOx-Based CCO Thin Films

Developing PLD growth and device-testing workflows for complex concentrated oxide memristor studies.

Dense YSZ Thin Films on Si via PLD

Linking process, texture, and thin-film properties for oriented YSZ films supporting glancing-angle architectures.

PEDOT:PSS Laser Writing and Nonlinear Microscopy

Quantifying conductivity changes and supporting nonlinear microscopy work in PEDOT:PSS-based materials systems.
