About

I am Zhaofu (Jeff) Chen, an undergraduate researcher at the University of California, Irvine, pursuing a B.S. in Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering with a minor in Materials Science and Engineering. My current and future research interests center on PEDOT/PEDOT:PSS and related organic semiconductor systems, especially how processing, doping, morphology, and mixed ionic-electronic transport shape device-level performance.

I am particularly interested in organic bioelectronics, flexible and transparent organic conductors, organic photovoltaics and photodetectors, and redox-active organic materials for safer, more sustainable batteries. This direction builds on my recent PEDOT:PSS laser-writing and nonlinear microscopy work, while carrying forward my training in thin-film characterization, electrochemical analysis, conductivity measurements, and materials data workflows.

Current Projects

PEDOT:PSS Laser Writing and Nonlinear Microscopy

Studying conductivity changes, formulation effects, and optical response in PEDOT:PSS-based polymer films for bioelectronic and nonlinear microscopy applications.

Organic Semiconductors and Mixed Conductors

Future-facing interest in PEDOT-family materials, organic mixed ionic-electronic conductors, and structure-processing-property maps for flexible organic electronic devices.

Organic Battery and Redox Polymer Materials

Exploring redox-active organic polymers and conjugated materials as tunable, sustainable candidates for electrochemical energy storage.

Organic Optoelectronics

Interest in conjugated polymers for organic photovoltaics, photodetectors, and transparent or flexible device architectures.

Amorphous High-Entropy Oxides for CO2 Electroreduction

Prior and continuing materials background: correlating amorphous structure, oxygen-vacancy chemistry, EIS response, and 4-probe conductivity across complex oxide thin films.

Indium-Oxide-Based Medium-Entropy Oxides

Mapping composition, processing, and phase stability using XRD indexing, quantitative phase analysis, and batch comparison workflows.
